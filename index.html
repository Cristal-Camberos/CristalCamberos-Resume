I'm currently a fourth year psychology student enrolled in the University of California Riverside. I transferred from Ventura College where I graduated with my Associate's Degree in psychology in 2018. At my time in Ventura College, I was involved in a variety of student run organizations such as Psychology Club and Queer Alliance. With these organizations I was able to learn various communication and leadership skills.

While involved with the Psychology Club, I was able to help organize several events to promote mental health for the student body. Our most popular event we held was the Clothesline Project which involved creating awareness for issues such as domestic abuse, child abuse, and sexual assault. We gathered resources and organizations around the community to provide information and support to those interested. We also were able to bring in guest speakers from these organizations to help either provide information on how to access their resources or to create safe spaces for students to engage with challenging topics such as sexual assault and rape culture.

While involved with Queer Alliance, I was able to help organize a student run panel for students and faculty to ask questions about how to be more accommodating for queer students in an academic setting. I was also able to help organize a Pride event for students to celebrate their queer identities and gather resources and support from local LGBTQIA+ organizations. Currently I'm volunteering from Crisis Text Line as a crisis counselor. There I am able to show empathy to those in crisis and help guide texters from a hot moment to a cooler one.
